How the formula works

This calculator uses the universal position-sizing formula that works on every asset class:

Quantity = (Equity × Risk%) ÷ |Entry − Stop|
TP1 = Entry ± Risk × 1 → 1:1 RR
TP2 = Entry ± Risk × 2 → 1:2 RR
TP3 = Entry ± Risk × 3 → 1:3 RR

Example: $1,000 equity, 1% risk, entry $4,310, stop $4,072 → Distance = 238, Risk = $10 → Position = 0.042 units (Order Value = $181), TP1 = $4,548 (+$10), TP2 = $4,786 (+$20), TP3 = $5,024 (+$30).

Pro tip: Scaling out at TP1 (50%), TP2 (30%), and TP3 (20%) locks in profit while letting winners run. This is the "tiered exit" used by professional traders.

How it works

A pip is the smallest price movement in a currency pair: 0.0001 for most pairs, 0.01 for JPY pairs.

A standard lot (100,000 units) on EUR/USD = $10/pip. Mini lot = $1/pip. Micro lot = $0.10/pip.

Zakat on Stocks

Zakat is due at 2.5% on net zakatable assets held above Nisab for one lunar year. Most scholars use the market value method for stocks. This calculator uses the silver nisab (more conservative).
